About Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist. He is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current (AC) electrical supply system.

Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia). He immigrated to the United States in 1884 and worked for renowned inventor Thomas Edison before establishing his own laboratories and developing numerous inventions and discoveries.

His groundbreaking work laid the foundation for many electrical technologies we use today, including the induction motor, the Tesla coil, and wireless power transmission. Tesla's ideas and inventions continue to inspire scientists, engineers, and inventors around the world.
